Data Scientist · ML Engineer · Istanbul, Türkiye

Hey. I'm Utku. I work with data, build ML systems, and care about where the output actually goes. Right now I'm doing an MS in Machine Learning at Georgia Tech.

I've spent time at Johnson & Johnson forecasting immune-cell growth for production planning, at Poiesy Biotech building prediction pipelines for bone marrow transplant outcomes, and at the United Nations University working on climate risk models with satellite and GIS data. These days I'm at Eurosis Consulting where I build ML and LLM tools that plug into real consulting work.

When I'm not doing that, I'm probably pointing a CNN at satellite imagery to find plastic in the ocean, or trying to figure out how wave energy distributes across open water using Sentinel-2 data. I like problems that sit at the edge of data and the real world.

I grew up in Istanbul. Curious kid, got restless early, started traveling and never really stopped. I hike through jungles, dive oceans, ride motorbikes across valleys, and do acrobatics on grass for no particular reason. I read history, Stoic and Zen philosophy, and anything that makes me rethink what I thought I already knew. I shoot what I see through my lens. I make music sometimes.

People ask what I'm like. I usually say I'm like an onion — rough on the outside, but it gets sweeter the deeper you go. Only a few get there though. Let your prejudice fade. Give it a shot.
